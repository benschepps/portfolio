How I Work & Show Up
My Approach
01
Understand First
Before anything gets written or planned, I spend time understanding the brand, the audience, and what the work is actually supposed to accomplish. That foundation changes everything that comes after.
02
System Over Post
A great post means nothing if the process behind it is chaos. I think in systems: calendars, workflows, prep checklists, approval docs. The infrastructure is part of the job.
03
Pull People In
Content that gets scrolled past is just noise. I am most interested in work that pulls people in and gives them something to respond to, share, or be part of.
04
Own It Fully
I take ownership. That means flagging problems before they become crises, raising my hand when something could be better, and delivering the work without someone having to follow up.
05
Live Environments Are My Best Work
Multi-day live production taught me something no classroom could: how to stay calm when things go sideways, communicate clearly under pressure, and make quick decisions without freezing.
06
I Show Up for the Team
My jobs have looked pretty different on the surface. The through-line has always been the same: figure out what the team needs, stay flexible, and treat the people around you well.
07
I Connect With a Lot of Different People
Kids, parents, athletes, executives, creative teams, field crews. My work has put me in front of a lot of different people and I have learned to adapt quickly. The variety is one of my favorite parts.
08
I Pay Attention to Who Is Not in the Room
A space only works when the people inside it feel like they genuinely belong. I pay attention to who is on the edges and how to bring them in. That takes intentional thought at every level.
